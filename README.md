##### &#x20;**What I Analyzed**



1\. Who manages each territory and where the stores are located

3\. Which stores are the top performers



4\. Who the top customers are in each territory



5\. Which product categories drive the most sales



6\. A marketing recommendation for next quarter



##### &#x20;**Key Findings**



\- Maryland generates more revenue than Massachusetts despite having fewer stores



\- Technology \& Accessories is by far the top revenue category in both territories



\- Store 736 in North Harford, MD is an extreme outlier it earns nearly $8.7M alone



\- Rewards program growth is a big opportunity many purchases are from non-rewards customers



\- Both territories spike in revenue during back to school season 



\*\*Maryland generates nearly double the revenue\*\* of Massachusetts — $11.45M vs $5.73M over the data period

\- \*\*Massachusetts customers spend more per transaction\*\* on average — $136.47 vs $128.00 in Maryland

\- \*\*Technology and Accessories\*\* is the top revenue category in both territories by a large margin

\- \*\*Store 736 (North Harford, MD)\*\* generated $8.7M alone — more than all 18 Massachusetts stores combined

\- \*\*89.6% of transactions have no rewards ID\*\* — meaning 9 out of 10 customers are unknown to the business

\- Both territories peak in revenue every \*\*fall season\*\* — October 2025 was the best month for both
